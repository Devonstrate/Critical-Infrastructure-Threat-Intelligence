# Critical Infrastructure Threat Intelligence Architecture

This repository serves as an open-source archive for formal, production-grade threat intelligence products and situational awareness bulletins developed during my tenure as Lead Graduate Assistant for Cyber Florida. These intelligence deliverables focus on real-time tracking of advanced persistent threats (APTs), emerging zero-day exploit perimeters, and strategic risk postures across global and regional critical infrastructure (CI) sectors.

## Operational Intelligence Objectives
* **TTP Profiling:** Mapping observed adversary campaigns directly against the MITRE ATT&CK framework to isolate initial access, kernel-level persistence, and lateral movement patterns.
* **Vulnerability Tracking:** Rapid synthesis of emerging CVEs, active exploit criteria, and patch management urgency for enterprise defense.
* **Defensive Mitigation:** Providing actionable indicators of compromise (IOCs), telemetry validation parameters, and technical guidance for security operations center (SOC) environments.

## 🗂️ Intelligence Deliverable Directory

### 1. Intel Report 2026-03: Iranian Cyber Counteroffensive Briefing
* **Core Focus:** This comprehensive threat assessment evaluates the decentralized Iranian cyber counteroffensive (Mosaic Defense Doctrine) activated in retaliation to joint U.S.-Israeli kinetic operations.
* **Key Vectors:** Details MuddyWater's deployment of AI-assisted malware families (GhostFetch, CHAR, HTTP_VIP), cloud-masked C2 infrastructure on Azure, and the use of commercial Starlink assets to maintain resilient connectivity. Tracks explicit high-priority exposures across Florida critical infrastructure surrounding MacDill AFB, including stolen electrical grid engineering schemas and physical psychological warfare operations.

### 2. Intel Bulletin 2026-01: AI Penetration and Edge Exploitation
* **Core Focus:** This bulletin analyzes the strategic emergence of automated AI penetration testing agents (ARTEMIS) capable of outperforming human specialists in multi-threaded network exploitation.
* **Key Vectors:** Tracks the coordinated exploitation of Microsoft SharePoint zero-days by Chinese-nexus groups, GRU-linked edge routing device compromises, and pro-Russian hacktivist targeting of operational technology (OT) SCADA interfaces.

### 3. Intel Bulletin 2026-02: Virtualization Flaws and Municipal Ransomware
* **Core Focus:** This intelligence product maps systemic vulnerabilities driven by the active exploitation of legacy VMware vCenter Server vulnerabilities and the persistent lurking of the Kimwolf botnet.
* **Key Vectors:** Details high-velocity ransomware-as-a-service (RaaS) targeting of critical supply chain aggregators (Ingram Micro) and cascading ransomware emergencies crippling municipal services, healthcare facilities (AZ Monica), and central 911 communication stacks.

### 4. Intel Bulletin 2026-03: OT Protocol Surge and Identity-First Vectors
* **Core Focus:** An evaluation of the high-intensity threat landscape defined by an 84% surge in attacks targeting unmanaged operational technology (OT) protocols like Modbus and EtherNet/IP.
* **Key Vectors:** Tracks CISA KEV additions for legacy Telnet server authentication bypasses, SolarWinds Web Help Desk deserialization flaws, and destructive "DynoWiper" malware campaigns deployed by Sandworm against decentralized renewable energy resource facilities.

### 5. Intel Bulletin 2026-04: Enterprise Resilience and Supply Chain Risk
* **Core Focus:** This delivery details the tactical shift of advanced persistent threat (APT) groups using "living off the land" techniques and identity loopholes to build persistent perimeters.
* **Key Vectors:** Analyzes the active exploitation of BeyondTrust privileged access perimeters, critical Ivanti Endpoint Manager Mobile code-injection zero-days, and the cascading impact of third-party ransomware outages on public safety networks and municipal billing infrastructure.

### 6. Intel Bulletin 2026-05: AI Weaponization and Kinetic Spillover
* **Core Focus:** An assessment of automated threat infrastructure, tracking a record low in adversary "breakout times" dropping to under 30 minutes due to AI-driven reconnaissance pipelines.
* **Key Vectors:** Profiles sophisticated supply chain injections targeting developer tools (the NX build tool and n8n platform), drone-enabled proximal wireless frame injections, and the Handala hack weaponizing Microsoft Intune administrative controls to execute destructive corporate wipes.

### 7. Intel Bulletin 2026-06: Pre-Positioning Tactics and Supply Chain Erosion
* **Core Focus:** Tracks conflict-driven pre-positioning by state-sponsored actors inside domestic networks and maximum-severity centralized management vulnerabilities.
* **Key Vectors:** Analyzes unauthenticated remote code execution (RCE) flaws in Cisco Secure Firewall Management Center (FMC) software exploited by the Interlock group, kernel-level persistence via BPFdoor implants, and security-prompt bypasses within automated cloud orchestration environments.

### 8. Intel Bulletin 2026-08: Industrialized Botnets and PLC Exploitation
* **Core Focus:** This blueprint analyzes the extensive, joint-agency investigation into Iranian-affiliated groups (Cyber Av3ngers and Ababil of Minab) actively exploiting internet-exposed programmable logic controllers (PLCs) across transit and water grids.
* **Key Vectors:** Details the industrialization of Chinese state-sponsored botnet infrastructure (KV Botnet), supply chain subversions targeting developer repositories (Bitwarden CLI and Checkmarx), and grid frequency stability risks documented by NERC regarding rapid AI data center load fluctuations.
